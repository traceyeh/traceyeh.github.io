21.11.2022

The Pacific had nothing on her

The way its salt droplets
Laid on her skin

The way the sun licked her thighs
And the sand stiched to her feet

The way when she laid on the rocks
They exhaled

The way the clouds shied away
And their shadows drew her silhouette

Not on land
But in the water.

The ocean was intimidated
By the way she spoke

How every word she said
Was thunder

How her breath was a melody
How the underwater turned into space

How she pierced through the sky
With her subtle gaze

How she cracked a joke
And the angels giggled.

The ocean shied at her feet
And as she waltzed in
It leaped to catch her

And the fish flapped their wings
And the birds sunk their beaks

And we all sat there
And held our breaths

Because comatose is natural
When the gods morph into human form

When all elements bow
Simply at the blink of her lashes.
